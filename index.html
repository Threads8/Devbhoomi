Winter Abodes

When the high Himalayan shrines close for winter due to heavy snowfall, the deities descend to their winter seats where worship continues.

Kharsali

Seat of Yamuna

The idol of Goddess Yamuna is brought here to the Shani Dev Temple in a grand procession.

Nov - Apr

Mukhba

Seat of Ganga

Goddess Ganga resides in the Ganga Temple here, near Harsil, amidst pristine apple orchards.

Nov - Apr

Ukhimath

Seat of Kedarnath

The Utsav Doli of Lord Shiva is seated in the Omkareshwar Temple during winter months.

Nov - May

Pandukeshwar

Seat of Badrinath

Lord Badri Vishal's Utsav Murti resides at Yog Dhyan Badri temple. The Rawal sits at Joshimath.

Nov - May

Important Advisory

Exact dates for Kedarnath and Badrinath are strictly determined by celestial calculations on Maha Shivratri and Basant Panchami respectively. Please verify with official authorities like BKTC before booking travel.

Beyond the Char Dham

Jewels of Garhwal

Explore the mystical confluences and sacred heights of the Garhwal region.

Hemkund Sahib

Highest Sikh Gurudwara

A star-shaped Gurudwara by a glacial lake, surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks. A test of faith and endurance.

Opens May
Wedding Destination

Triyuginarayan

Celestial Union

The sacred site where Lord Shiva married Goddess Parvati. An eternal flame (Akhand Dhuni) has been burning here for three yugas.

Kartik Swami

Crown of Garhwal

Perched at 3,050m, this temple dedicated to Lord Kartikeya offers a mesmerizing 360-degree view of the Himalayan peaks.

Panch Prayag

Five Holy Confluences

Vishnuprayag, Nandaprayag, Karnaprayag, Rudraprayag, and Devprayag. Where the Alaknanda meets tributaries to form the Ganga.

Tungnath

Highest Shiva Temple

Part of the Panch Kedar, located at 3,680m. A moderate trek offering stunning views of Nanda Devi and Chowkhamba.

Dhari Devi

Guardian of Char Dham

Located on the banks of Alaknanda. It is mandatory to pay respects here for a successful and safe yatra.
